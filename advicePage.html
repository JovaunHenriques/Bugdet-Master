  • Get Started

    • General Rules

    1. Emergency Fund: Allocate a portion of your leftover income to build an emergency fund. This fund should ideally cover 3-6 months' worth of living expenses. Aim to reach at least $500 in your emergency fund initially.
    2. Debt Repayment: If you have any high-interest debt, such as credit card debt, consider allocating a portion of your leftover income to pay it off more quickly. Aim to reach a balance of $500 for this purpose.
    3. Savings Goals: Identify short-term and long-term savings goals, such as saving for a vacation, a down payment on a house, or retirement. Allocate a portion of your leftover income towards these goals. Aim to reach $2000 for a mid-term savings goal, such as a vacation or a major purchase.
    4. Retirement Savings: Contribute to retirement accounts such as a 401(k) or an IRA. Retirement savings should be a priority, so allocate a significant portion of your leftover income towards this goal. Aim to reach $4000 for long-term retirement savings.
    5. Investing: Consider investing a portion of your leftover income in stocks, bonds, or other investment vehicles to help grow your wealth over the long term. If you're new to investing, you may want to start with low-risk options or seek guidance from a financial advisor.
    6. Review and Adjust: Regularly review your budget and savings plan to ensure you're on track to meet your financial goals. Adjust your allocations as needed based on changes in your income, expenses, or financial priorities.
    7. Automate Savings: Set up automatic transfers or deposits to your savings and investment accounts to make saving easier and more consistent.
    8. Monitor Spending: Keep track of your expenses and look for opportunities to reduce unnecessary spending. Cutting back on non-essential purchases can free up more money to put towards savings and investments.
    9. Seek Professional Advice: If you're unsure about how to best allocate your leftover income or how to achieve your financial goals, consider seeking advice from a financial planner or advisor.
    • Leftover Income $500 or Less: Prioritize essentials: Ensure that your basic needs such as housing, utilities, groceries, and transportation are covered first. Cut discretionary spending: Review your non-essential expenses such as dining out, entertainment, and shopping. Look for areas where you can cut back to free up more money for savings. Look for additional income opportunities: Consider picking up extra work or finding ways to increase your income to improve your financial situation.
    • Leftover Income $1000: Build an emergency fund: If you haven't already, focus on building an emergency fund with at least $1000. This fund can provide a financial buffer for unexpected expenses. Pay off high-interest debt: Allocate a portion of your leftover income towards paying off any high-interest debt to reduce interest payments and improve your financial health. Start saving for short-term goals: Begin saving for short-term goals such as a vacation or a major purchase. Set aside a portion of your leftover income specifically for these goals.
    • Leftover Income $2000: Increase retirement contributions: If you haven't already maxed out your retirement account contributions, consider allocating more of your leftover income towards retirement savings. Invest for the long term: Consider investing a portion of your leftover income in long-term investment vehicles such as stocks, bonds, or mutual funds to build wealth over time. Review insurance coverage: Review your insurance policies (health, life, auto, etc.) to ensure you have adequate coverage. Allocate funds towards increasing coverage if necessary.
